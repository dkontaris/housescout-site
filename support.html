Getting started

  • Add a property. In Safari, Rightmove, Zoopla or OnTheMarket, tap Share and choose HouseScout. The app saves the link and fills in the details. You can also tap + in the app and paste a link.
  • Set your criteria. Open Settings (the slider icon) to set your budget, add the schools you care about, and add your offices for commute times.
  • Read the verdict. Each property shows a Strong, Maybe or No, and tapping it reveals the plain-English reasons behind the score.

Sharing with a partner

Turn on iCloud sync in Settings, then choose Sharing → Share this list… and send the invite. Once they accept, you both add to and see the same list, kept in sync.

Frequently asked

Why does a card say “Fetching listing…”?

No, the app is reading the page in the background. It usually fills in within a few seconds, then shows the photo, price and verdict.

Which property sites work best?

OnTheMarket usually reads in full. Rightmove and Zoopla render differently, so the app pulls in the title and photo and you can fill in any missing facts yourself.

How is commute time worked out?

It's an approximate, offline estimate based on straight-line distance, handy for comparing properties at a glance, not a live train timetable. Anything over two hours to your nearest office is treated as an automatic “No”.

Why does the catchment note mention exams?

The distance-based catchment score applies to comprehensives and primaries. Selective grammar schools admit on entrance-exam rank rather than distance, so the app flags that so you never misread the score.

Does my data sync or leave my phone?

Only if you choose. iCloud sync is off by default; with it off, everything stays on your device. See the Privacy Policy for the full picture.

Is HouseScout free?

Yes. Free, with no ads and no in-app purchases.
