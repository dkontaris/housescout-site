Privacy Policy

Last updated: 1 July 2026

The short version: HouseScout has no accounts, no analytics, and no advertising. We don't collect, sell, or share your personal data. Your saved properties and criteria live on your iPhone, and only leave it if you choose to turn on your own iCloud.

This policy explains what HouseScout (“the app”, “we”) does with information when you use it. It's written to be read, not to hide things.

What the app stores

When you save a property, the app keeps the following on your device:

  • The listing link you shared or pasted, and the details it reads from that page (such as price, photo, address, bedrooms, floor area and tenure).
  • Your house-hunting criteria: budget, the schools and offices you add, and any notes you type.
  • Distances and a fit score the app calculates from the above.

This is held in the app's private storage on your iPhone. We, the developer, have no access to it.

iCloud sync and sharing (optional, off by default)

If you turn on iCloud sync in Settings, your saved data is stored in your own private iCloud account (using Apple's CloudKit) so it stays in step across your devices. If you choose to share your list with another person, it syncs to them through Apple's CloudKit sharing.

In both cases the data is handled by Apple under Apple's Privacy Policy and tied to the iCloud accounts involved. The developer never receives or sees this data.

Network requests the app makes

  • Reading a listing. To pull in the price, photo and facts, the app fetches the web page of the link you shared, directly from that property website (for example Rightmove, Zoopla or OnTheMarket). The app identifies itself honestly as HouseScout when it does this. Those websites may record the visit in their own logs; their privacy practices are their own.
  • Working out distances. The app uses Apple's geocoding service to turn an address into a map location, so it can show distance and approximate commute time. Apple processes that lookup under its privacy policy.
  • Searching for a place. When you type a school or office into Settings or the setup wizard, the text you type is sent to Apple's Maps service to suggest matching places, the same way the Maps app works. Apple processes those searches under its privacy policy.
  • Your location (optional). If you tap "Current Location" when adding a place, the app asks for one-time permission and uses your location only to fill in that address. It stays on your device, is never tracked in the background, and you can always type an address instead.

The app does not send your data to the developer or to any third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking service.

What we collect

Nothing. There are no developer accounts, no sign-in, no analytics SDKs, no advertising identifiers, and no third-party trackers in HouseScout. We don't build a profile of you and we have no server that receives your activity.

Children

HouseScout is a general-purpose house-hunting tool and is not directed at children.

Changes

If this policy changes, we'll update this page and the “last updated” date above. Material changes will be reflected before they take effect in a new version of the app.
